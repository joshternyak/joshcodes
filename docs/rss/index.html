More traffic.

More sales.

Better copy.

Keyword Optimize Your Copy

Before you get started on writing any copy, know this:

If you're not optimizing your copy for a certain keyword, search engines won't fully understand what you're trying to rank for. When writing copy for your online business, focus on planning and optimizing before you start writing. - Josh Ternyak

You should know how to:

  1. Find keywords
  2. Choose the right keywords
  3. Use keyword research tools
  4. And More

For example, when I wrote "The 9 Best Carpet Cleaners to Buy in 2020", I targeted the keyword "best carpet cleaners". I did for several reasons:

  1. The keyword "best carpet cleaner" is searched for over 20,000 times every month.
  2. People need to keep their carpets clean
  3. Carpet cleaners are expensive
  4. I'll get a cut of the cost of each vacuum every time someone buys it through the affiliate link.

This thoughtful planning is one of the most important things you can do when writing copy for your online business.

The MOST important thing you should do when writing is...

Write To Make Money

When I first started reviewing cleaning products, I wasn't familiar with writing to make money.

I WAS familiar with writing to get a good grade in school. After my first review, I started to understand how to write to make money and sell other company's products.

When you are writing copy for your website, you must do so in a way that brings customers down your sales funnel. If you don't have a sales funnel, you must make one.

Here's a great video simply explaining sales funnels by Russell Brunson:

A great book that goes deeper into sales funnels called The Underground Playbook. It is critical that you write in a way that leads readers and potential customers to buy your product, or other people's products.

If you're an affiliate marketer or writer, writing to make money is also necessary.

The biggest mistake I have made in copywriting was to write content that didn't lead to lots of sales. When I made my first HTML website theme, LoveYourLanding, I knew I had a goal of selling the theme to many developers.

So, I decided to write a post on IndieHackers that got a good amount of reads, around 7,000.

This might seem like I wrote a fancy post that drove lots of traffic to my website theme. It drove some traffic, but didn't lead to many sales.

What I will do differently next time I launch a product is to put more time into marketing it, and less time into building it.

The problem is, when you have a product, building it is not nearly as important as marketing it. Hear me out. We can agree that building a valuable product is important.

However, if you built a product that nobody ends up buying, then, the time spent building the product was mostly waste.

This is why I started this blog and podcast, to teach web developers and entrepreneurs my mistakes and what I've learned from them so they won't make the same mistakes.

When you write, write to make money. Focus on marketing your product more than building it. Grow an audience before you spend lots of time building your product. You'll get more traffic, attention, and most importantly...sales.

Write Organized Copy

As you're reading this, you might have noticed that the paragraphs are short. The sentences are also short. This helps you, the reader, not get bored and tired of reading long paragraphs.

Having images in your copy is great, but only if they make your copy more useful. For example, having an image of a fish in an article about growing an online business isn't useful.

You might be distracting your readers when you use random images. Instead, work on improving your copy by only using images that help the reader understand your message.

Brian Dean is a copywriting expert that has many videos you should watch. Here's one of them:

In short, focus on writing short paragraphs, and sentences that are compact.

Know Your End Goal

What's the end goal of your copy? Is it to drive traffic to your website? Is it to grow your audience? Or is it to get more customers?

You must know how to write copy that gets you to your end goal. Having a combination of copy to drive traffic to your website, grow your audience, and get more customers is your best bet.

Get To The Point

One of the main reasons why Brian Dean is so successful in SEO and copywriting is because he gets to the point, fast. He writes actionable advice that works, and doesn't include any "fluff" in his content.

This is how every copywriter should write. Readers don't have time to read through your content's "fluff". They want actionable advice that makes them more money or teaches them something valuable.

Describe The Benefits Of Buying The Product

Reviewing vacuums and stain removers is valuable. People need to keep their carpets and floors clean. Well, they also don't have lots of time to read.

How is it possible to write valuable copy if people have very short attention spans?

The answer: write copy that describes the benefits of buying the product.

For example, if you're writing about a website theme, focus on writing how the website theme will make the potential customer more money.

If you're writing about cleaning products, focus on what the reader will have when they buy the product. I do this a constantly in my reviews:

Write copy that is simple, organized, and with an end goal in mind. Describing the benefits of buying the product is an easy way to get more readers to become your customers.

Don't Just Talk About Your Product

Instead, show people what your product can do. For example, if you're building a website theme to sell online, don't just write about what the theme does.

You should make a video showing you going through the buying process of the theme. When you record yourself "buying" the theme, show how fast it takes for you to make a website with the theme.

This is what I didn't do when I was building LoveYourLanding. The next time I am working on making an online product, I will focus on 3 things:

  1. Getting more traffic
  2. Writing better copy
  3. Show visitors what my product can do

If you don't know how to make a professional website for your business, hire someone that knows how to do it.

Reduce Any Price Objections

Adding the term "a small" before any price makes a huge increase in conversions, according to a study at the University of Pittsburgh.

For example, if you're selling ice cream online, you might be used to labeling them with only their prices.

So, for the chocolate chip ice cream, you might add a label of "$5 fee".

Instead, label the price "A small $5 fee".

This will boosts your conversions for the products you sell, are writing about, or are advertising.

When you work in a messy environment, you feel messy. On the other hand, when you work in a clean and organized environment, you're more likely to be more productive. You'll also feel physically better because of the minimized dust.

3. Work in a quiet environment

Imagine trying to focus in a bar. The loud music and people will distract you and prevent you from being able to focus on your work. The same holds true for your work environment at home.

If you live by yourself, this shouldn't be a problem. However, if you live with family or a roommate, tell them you are working and just kindly ask them to not turn on loud music or television.

Don't try to learn everything at once

The biggest mistake I've made throughout my web development journey was to try to learn everything at once. When you try to learn everything at once, you end up learning little or even nothing.

As you're learning, don't jump from tutorial to tutorial. Have the video tutorial or blog tutorial on full screen to help minimize any possible distractions.

Set a specific amount of time that you'll learn everyday and stick to it. It's better to consistently learn 1 hour everyday than to learn 3 hours on Monday and 10 minutes on Thursday, according to the study by leadaz.org.

My story

2018: It was the last day of school. I came home, and my brother welcomed me and said, "Congratulations! Now, you'll have so much time on your hands." I replied, "What should I do with it", and he said, "You should teach yourself how to code".

This was the most memorable moment of my life. And so that's what I did. I spent the entire summer watching youtube videos and reading articles on the basics of HTML, CSS, JS, and everything in between. The first few days were exciting and exhausting.

I remember sitting on my bedroom floor for over 5 hours being extremely confused on how to get rid of default margin on the page. After finding the solution, I was the happiest web developer alive. All I had to do was body { margin: 0 } in the stylesheet. It was on that day that I knew I found my hidden talent and passion.

I spent the next 2 months applying my skills by building landing pages. But there was one day when I didn't feel like coding at all. I was tired of the debugging, the CSS errors, and the images not loading.

So, I did what I never thought could be possible in the world of web development. I took a BREAK. It was the most important choice I had made that taught me an important lesson. To be a successful web developer, you must take breaks. What works for me might not work for you, but I usually take a 5 minute break every 1–2 hours.

2019: After 7 months of learning the basics of web development and building landing pages, I stepped into the world of client work. Actually, I took a leap into it, and texted my friends asking them, "Hey, can you ask your dad or mom if they need or want a website". Funny enough, it worked. I ended up building a portfolio for my friend's dad that showcased his photography. At the end of that project, I learned 3 valuable lessons.

  1. Before you begin working on a project, plan everything you will need and do.
  2. Constant client communication is critical if you want to build trust.
  3. To prevent future headaches, know how to do what is necessary to get the job done.

After this project, I decided to build my portfolio. At first, it didn't look that nice, and so I decided to use a free HTML theme to save time building it. After it was nice and filled with my projects, I focused the next two months on learning backend programming. This included the basics of python and web development with flask. It was a challenging process, but in the end, I had managed to successfully build twitter replica called "BlogItPoints". However, it taught me a lot about how the web works, and was also a fun experience.

At this point in my web developer life, I finally reached a level of satisfaction. I understood how much work I put into reaching my goal, and in fact reached that goal. I taught myself the basics of code, how to build websites, and how to find people to build them for. This is when I decided to build my own CSS framework from scratch. I planned everything I would do, and I estimated that it would take me around 2 weeks to build. It ended up taking me only two 2 days. This brings me to another lesson I've learned:

It's better to overestimate how long it will take you to complete a project and finish it earlier than expected, rather than underestimating how long it will take, and not meeting the deadline.

2020: When I came back home, I was ready to build a product that would save developers and designers hundreds of hours of time so they can focus on building web products that make the world a better place. And so that's what I did. I spent 113 long hours building LoveYourLanding full of blood, sweat, and tears (I got a paper cut).

I hope you feel inspired, motivated, and excited to make your own web development dreams come true. Becoming a self-taught web developer is hard, especially when you're young.

But, if you put in the time, effort, and thinking, then you will achieve what seems impossible, especially if you use LoveYourLanding to give you a head start. I'm 15 and taught myself how to code and be an independent web developer when I was only 13, so don't make excuses.
