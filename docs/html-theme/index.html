I Just Finished My First HTML Theme. Here’s What I’ve Learned.

Web Development • May 07, 2020

Recently, I finished coding my first HTML theme for BuildFaster. It is a website (hasn’t launched yet) where I will sell high quality and easy-to-use HTML/CSS themes and templates. No matter if it's your 1st or 58th time building a website, BuildFaster will save you hours of designing and coding time. Well-documented and easy-to-follow code makes the whole process smoother than ever before.


About The Theme

The HTML theme is called, “Lightning Blue”. I gave it that name because the blog pops out of the design very well at the beginning. Using it to lead users to clicking on certain parts of the site.


So, what did I learn?

This whole process has been a huge learning experience for me. Among all of the things I’ve learned, the ones that stuck out the most were

  • Making a website consistent
  • Dedication. Don’t stop
  • How to pass the Google Lighthouse test with flying colors

So, what are you waiting for? Let’s get started! 🏃‍♂️


Making a website consistent

Maybe one of the smaller things I learned (because I already knew this) but I still wanted to tell you about it. Make your website consistent! Don’t switch up the style between different sections on your page. That will make it look like you’re on a whole new site. Trust me. This is important.

This principle is even more important for me because I am creating a theme. The theme is consistent. Your user should get the same feeling throughout the whole theme. For example, the content sections for each part of the site are very similar. Dark heading, lighter text. Images the same width. For the blog post template, it is very standard and looks like what you would see on the internet. You will not regret keeping your website consistent.


Dedication. Don’t stop.

I’m slightly embarrassed when telling you this one 😳. I’ve honestly never built a complete website (excluding website portfolios) from start to finish. Strange right? I’ve always had trouble with sticking to one project and not quitting if I find a flaw or I am attracted by a completely new idea. So for this project I really had to “just keep swimming”, if you know what I mean.


I’m not joking on this one. It is so important! Over the past few years of learning web development, I’m willing to bet that I’ve given up on more amazing ideas than I can count on my fingers (that’s 10 if you didn’t know). I am to this day still disappointed by that. Who knows where I would be right now if I kept going with even one or two of those ideas?

At certain points when building this theme, I wanted to quit again. Either the navigation drop down menu wasn’t opening, the animations were out of sync, or something else that made my brain go boom 💥 would stop me from continuing the project I was working on!

I went by the motto “keep on keeping on” and pushed through it. Once I finished debugging a certain piece of the problem, the “weight on my back” felt lighter. This inspired me to finish the theme and boy did it feel good to push the final commits to my GitHub repository.


How to pass the Google Lighthouse test with flying colors

Why should you care about getting a good website audit score? Wait ✋.


Getting a good score on the Google Lighthouse test is very important! There are 5 criteria:

  1. Performance
  2. Accessibility
  3. Best Practices
  4. SEO
  5. Progressive Web App (optional)

The performance report (in my humble opinion) is the most useful piece of advice you can get from Google Lighthouse. It helped me out a ton with rendering speed and making my site optimized for different devices. I learned how to minify CSS and JavaScript files, use proper images sizes, and also found a cheat for optimizing package size of FontAwesome’s icons (fontello.com).

In the end, my scores improved from the 60s and 70s, all the way to the high 90s and 100s.

Wrapping Up

So, to conclude, I want you to know that throughout building this theme, I have learned a ton of helpful lessons to carry on into my future themes and other experiences in the future.

I will be selling these themes by the end of May 2020. To stay updated, you should follow BuildFaster.io on IndieHackers to get the word of the latest themes, features, or other fun ideas I have in store for the future.

I hope you apply these concepts into your life, whether it’s in web development or something completely different!
